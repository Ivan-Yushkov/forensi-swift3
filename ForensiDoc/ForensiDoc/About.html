Users of ForensiDoc should be familiar and apply relevant statutes, codes and regulatory professional guidelines concerning documentation of personal data and consent to the recording of such data within their own jurisdiction.

ForensiDoc®

ForensiDoc® is an App that is designed to allow secure recording and documentation of health, forensic, or scene assessments in a wide variety of settings. It has been designed to allow simple capture of all relevant information and outputting it as an editable Word document with accompanying hand-drawn diagrams, images, audio and video files. The App uses the drawing, camera and recording facilities of the device being used. It is intended for use primarily by healthcare and forensic practitioners who may be working independently or in a range of settings, both indoors and outdoors. However ForensiDoc® can also be used by lay people without health, scientific or technical knowledge to record data in settings (such as road traffic collisions, witnessing possible criminal incidents) where immediacy and accuracy of recording are needed. The App allows consent to be sought for a range of functions. Such data can be used as evidence in a variety of legal, judicial and tribunal settings.

Data Capture

Data is entered in text or radio-button format. Completion of a number of fields are compulsory (identified by orange buttons). Each piece of data entered is required to be saved before proceeding to the next field. For the healthcare professional a range of standardised, validated, scoring systems are incorporated which can assist in assessing assorted health conditions including mental health, drug dependence and alcohol dependence. The App can capture data about injuries, scenes, implements, locations, damage to property, geo-locating and accurately timing it.

Security

The data is not accessible to others within the electronic setting as it is uploaded and encrypted before being converted into a downloadable, editable Word file. The App encrypts the information and preserves the chain of custody from collection to output of data.
